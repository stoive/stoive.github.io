sessionStorage and localStorage

Always wanted to persist data between page loads, without having to use some hack (like posting it back to the server, or putting it in location.hash)?

sessionStorage is a key-value pair dictionary that can be accessed by pages from the same domain over the period of the browser session. It'll stick around through clicking links, back/forward navigation, and refreshing the page. 





	localStorage is much the same, however the data remains in storage until it
	is explicitly removed - close the tab or browser, or reboot the machine, and
	the data will still be there.
